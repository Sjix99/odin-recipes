Lasagna

Description

This is a lasagna recipe. A traditional Italian dish.

Ingredients

  • Meat Sauce:
    • Ground beef
    • Italian sausage
    • Tomatoes
    • Italian Seasoning
  • Cheese filling
    • Ricotta cheese
    • Egg
    • Parmesan cheese
    • Herbs and seasonings
  • Pasta and topping
    • Lasagna noodles
    • Mozzarella cheese

Steps

      Step 1: Make the meat sauce

    1. Brown your choice of ground meat, such as beef, pork, or a combination of the two, in a large pot or skillet.
    2. Drain the excess fat from the pan.
    3. Stir in your tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, along with your seasonings, such as Italian herbs, garlic, salt, and pepper.
    4. Let the sauce simmer for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to combine.

      2: Prepare the cheese mixture

    1. In a separate bowl, combine ricotta cheese with egg, grated Parmesan, and herbs like fresh parsley or basil.
    2. Mix until all the ingredients are well combined.

      Step 3: Cook the noodles

    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    2. Cook the lasagna noodles until they are al dente, meaning they are slightly firm to the bite. If using no-boil noodles, you can skip this step.
    3. Drain the noodles and lay them flat on a clean towel or parchment paper to prevent them from sticking together.

      Step 4: Assemble the lasagna

    1. Preheat your oven to 350–375°F (175–190°C).
    2. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish to prevent the noodles from sticking.
    3. Arrange a single layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
    4. Spread about one-third of the ricotta cheese mixture evenly over the noodles.
    5. Spoon a layer of meat sauce over the cheese.
    6. Sprinkle with shredded mozzarella cheese.
    7. Repeat these layers until you've used all of your ingredients, typically creating two to three full layers. Finish with a final layer of noodles topped with the remaining meat sauce and plenty of mozzarella cheese.

      Step 5: Bake and rest

    1. Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil to trap the moisture and ensure the noodles cook through.
    2. Bake for 30–50 minutes, or until the sauce is bubbly.
    3. Remove the foil and continue baking for another 10–15 minutes, or until the cheese on top is melted and golden brown.
    4. Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it rest for at least 15 minutes before serving. This allows the layers to set and makes it much easier to slice.
